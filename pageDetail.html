Scenes from the Pebble Beach Tour d'Elegance

The Pebble Beach Tour d'Elegance, now in its 18th year, is an annual tour that adds an extra element of elegance and pageantry offering all Pebble Beach Concours entrants the opportunity to prove their magnificent vehicles are not only stunning display pieces but also functioning automobiles. The tour traces portions of the original 17-Mile Drive, traverses nearby mountains and valleys, and pauses for a time in charming Carmel-by-the-Sea.

"SOMETHING FUNDAMENTAL HAS HAPPENED TODAY BECAUSE IT WAS A COMPLETELY DIFFERENT CAR TO THE ONE WE HAD IN FREE PRACTICE."

After a brief rain in the early morning hours, the skies had cleared for the traditional Thursday tour. The sun was shining brightly as the exotic automobiles lined up at the starting area, despite forecasts of more rain later in the day. Race legend Brian Redman was among the drivers for the special drive.

Participants looped through the 17-Mile Drive and made a return back along Route 1 before heading toward Tehama Golf Club and challenging tight turns on Carmel Valley Road. After stopping at Carmel-by-the-Sea for lunch, the cars continued down Carmel Valley Road to Highway 1 and concluded at the 18th fairway at Pebble Beach where they would be judged on Sunday. If an automobile completed the Tour, it would receive extra consideration in the case of a tie in class on Sunday.

While rain came late in the afternoon after the event was over, some of the featured cars were the 1953 Ferrari 250 MM, 1955 Mercedes-Benz 300 SL, 1967 Maserati Mistral Spyder, and 1972 BMW 3.0 CSL. The 2022 Pebble Beach Concours d'Elegance is the pinnacle event for many car collectors around the world.

"IT WAS A TOUGH SESSION. THERE WAS ONLY A VERY SMALL WINDOW TO GET TO THE LAP TIME I NEEDED."

We interviewed Mark Higgins for his part in setting the current racing lap for the inaugural course. Mark stated he was excited about the modifications to the car this year, and that the new tires performed exceptionally well under the challenging conditions. "These new compounds are almost too sticky for the car," he laughed.

According to race officials, modifications to the new course included a slight kink at the entry to turn three, additional run-off areas around turns 5 and 6, and a resurfaced exit to the hairpin before the final straight. "All of these changes contribute to a much safer experience for the drivers," stated official Mark Thompson.

"I THINK THERE'S STILL A LITTLE MORE TO COME FROM THE CAR TOO"

In summary, the Tour de Elegance proved once again that it's not just about static beauty but also dynamic performance. With sunshine highlighting these automotive treasures against the stunning Pacific coastline, spectators enjoyed a rare chance to see these masterpieces in their natural element. Sunday's main Concours d'Elegance promises to be spectacular, weather permitting.
